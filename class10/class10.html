XRay <- sum(as.numeric(gsub(',','', pdb_stats$X.ray)))

# Perc EM

[1] 13582  2287  4181   132     9     0

n_total <- sum(as.numeric(gsub(',','', pdb_stats$Total)))

Now we have the summed up values for both the X-ray and EM columns, so we may now divide it by the total amount in the dataset.

(XRay) / n_total
[1] 0.8412978
(EM) / n_total
[1] 0.09213745

Now let’s add both values together and find the total percentage.

(XRay + EM) / n_total
[1] 0.9334352

The percentage of structures in the PDB which are solved by X-ray and Electron Microscopy is about 93.34%.

Q4. Water molecules normally have 3 atoms. Why do we see just one atom per water molecule in this structure?

It is a result of the x-ray resolution that we only see one atom per water molecule rather than three. This makes it so we only see the oxygen molecule and not the two hydrogen molecules.
